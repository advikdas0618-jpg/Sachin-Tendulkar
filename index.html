Sachin Tendulkar

Tendulkar took up cricket at the age of eleven, made his Test match debut on 15 November 1989 against Pakistan in Karachi at the age of sixteen, and went on to represent Mumbai domestically and India internationally for over 24 years. In 2002, halfway through his career, Wisden ranked him the second-greatest Test batsman of all time, behind Don Bradman, and the second-greatest ODI batsman of all time, behind Viv Richards. The same year, Tendulkar was a part of the team that was one of the joint-winners of the 2002 ICC Champions Trophy. Later in his career, Tendulkar was part of the Indian team that won the 2011 Cricket World Cup, his first win in six World Cup appearances for India. He had previously been named "Player of the Tournament" at the 2003 World Cup.

Sachin Tendulkar, often referred to as the "God of Cricket," is one of the greatest cricketers in the history of the sport. Born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India, he became a global icon through his incredible cricketing achievements. Tendulkar made his debut for India at the young age of 16 and went on to break numerous records throughout his career. He is the highest run-scorer in the history of both Test and One-Day International (ODI) cricket, with over 34,000 runs and 100 international centuries to his name.

His remarkable consistency and skill as a batsman earned him the admiration of fans worldwide. Tendulkar has received numerous prestigious awards, including the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan, and was honoured with the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award, in 2014. His contributions to Indian cricket are immeasurable, and he continues to inspire young cricketers around the world. After retiring from international cricket in 2013, Tendulkar remains a prominent figure in the sport and actively engages in charity work, making him a true legend, not only in cricket but also in the hearts of millions.
